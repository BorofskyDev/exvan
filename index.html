exile

A hopeful cynic's take on deconstruction, deconversion, life, hope, and so many other things.

ABOUT

I am a geriatric millennial. I was raised Southern Baptist and even attended two different SBC seminaries. I was also raised in an incredibly conservative home and grew up on Rush Limbaugh, Sean Hannity, Bill O’Reilly, and others. I abandoned most of my conservative views sometime in my mid-20s, but only began questioning my faith over a decade later. I am now an adventurer trying to make sense out of the senselessness of life.

When deconstructing you will find yourself vacillating between two seemingly opposite feelings, but paradoxically they are the same. First, you will feel the euphoria of freedom, of a life that is limited by hope of Heaven or fear of Hell, a life where you can properly choose who you want to be. Second, you will feel utterly, completely lost and alone, but this feeling only exists because of the first.


For some, to leave their faith is a rather simple act. Their careers aren’t dependent on their faith, their familial relations exist outside of a faith structure, their community exists outside of their faith, in short, their faith is an add-on to their life. To others, such as myself, their faith is their life. To abandon their faith is to abandon all that they are, all that they have, everything they’ve been built up to be. Tt is not just a deconstruction of their beliefs, it’s a deconstruction of their entire identity as a human being.


I was aiming to be the next big thing in Christianity. I truly believed in Christianity, even if at times I questioned things and pushed the limits. I am a former seminary student, someone who wanted to be an evangelist, then a pastor, and then a professor of Christian philosophy. I wanted to save this world for Christ. I rubbed shoulders with people who now advise politicians, I was friends with men who went on to be pastors who have held rallies for Donald Trump and had him in their pulpits. And now, midway through my life, I am left sifting through the pieces of a me that I fully destroyed, trying to discover which parts are a part of who I am, and which parts were thrust on me.


Where do I go from here?


Well, I guess that is the entire point of this blog. When I was a Christian I wrote. For over a decade I ran a blog that at times would bring in a few thousand visitors a day, but it’s unlikely you or anyone you know heard of it (or have heard of me). I won’t link to it because it’s inactive and some spam service has taken over the domain site. Over a decade of writings are gone and I couldn’t be happier. It’s rather poetic, in a way. Writing was an escape for me, a way to wrestle with my thoughts. Leaving the faith took that from me because I didn’t want others to find out that I was abandoning my faith. Most still don’t know, at least not officially, though I suspect that they suspect my agnosticism. Such is the power of fundamentalist faith.


What this isn't


This isn’t a blog about how evil Christianity is or how people are stupid for believing it. Personally, I have a soft spot for religious people who found their religion in love and who live it, and that includes Christians. I might point out some inconsistencies in the arguments Christians use to defend their faith, but it won’t be a focal point because I’m just not interested in that aspect of living.


This isn’t a place where I will air my grievances against Christians all the time, though it will happen. This isn’t some hardcore atheist blog where I’ll explore all the dirty details of Christianity “behind the scenes.” That’s been done so much that at this point it’s a trope. It’d be like making another origin story for a superhero – yeah, we get it, get on with the story already.


What this is


I want this to be a spot where I share my thoughts on life, Christianity, the ongoings of the world, and maybe a little hope sprinkled in for good measure. This is me processing my beliefs and experiences. I do have what is slowly being accepted as “religious trauma,” I did endure quite a bit and I might explore some of that. To be honest, however, there are a lot of painful memories that I’d rather remain memories rather than part of a memorandum.


When I wrote before I obsessed over how many people would view my site, one posting everyday so I could get more clicks. I wanted a big name, I wanted attention, I wanted to be someone. Now, I’ve built my own website and didn’t put any site tracking on it because I just don’t care. I haven’t plastered my name all over, though I have left an image to show that I am, in fact, a real person. It’s a semi-anonymous site (though it wouldn’t take Sherlock Holmes to discover who I am – I have my picture on here…).


This is, in short, my therapy. It’s me getting back into writing, on sharing my thoughts. You’re welcome to read and agree, disagree, laugh, cry, or whatever the fuck you wish. Perhaps you’re someone who is facing that dilemma of knowing you can’t live your life as a Christian (or Muslim, or Jew, or any other fundamentalist branch of any other religion). Perhaps you’re beginning to find both fear and delight in that feeling of freedom. Through these writings I hope you will see that while you might be in exile, you are not alone. You might find yourself in the wilderness of doubt, of lost identity, know that ultimately you not an aimless wanderer but a brave explorer who has finally embarked upon the greatest adventure in which anyone can partake; the discovery of you.
